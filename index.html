Rice Cooking Calculator

Tired of getting mushy or dry rice? Use calculator below to cook better rice consistently.

Directions

About

The "first-knuckle" method works... until you get a pot that is too wide or rice that isn't too forgiving with water margins.

I've since moved on from that method and went with a more consistent approach: using a kitchen scale.

How to use

First get a cheap digital kitchen scale (eg. CDR King, Lazada) then measure the following:

  • the weight of one rice cup (takal or gou/Âêà). A properly calibrated scale should give about 150g. Note that a rice cup is smaller than a standard measuring "cup".
  • the weight of your rice cooker pot.
  • (if possible) the weight of your typical amount of cooked rice. Subtract the pot weight and divide by the number of people eating to get the average

Enter those values to the calculator along with the following:

  • the average rice consumed by each person in your group. This is usually 200g - 300g so 250g is a good guess.
  • the ideal water-to-rice ratio. For normal white rice, this should be 1.5. For brown rice and similar varieties, or old rice, use a higher value like 2.0.
  • the weight of leftover rice, if any

Press Calculate to calculate the ideal values for the weight of uncooked rice as well as the whole pot with water included.

Troubleshooting

Too much water? Lower the ratio by 0.1 - 0.2 next time. Do the reverse if the rice is too dry.

Not enough rice? Increase the average rice or add extra people to the count.
